Collision Repair FAQ

Signs of structural damage include doors that don't close properly, uneven panel gaps, pulling when driving straight, and visible frame bends. After any moderate-to-severe collision, a professional structural inspection is critical — cosmetic damage often hides more serious frame issues.
Collision coverage covers repairs from accidents (minus your deductible). Comprehensive handles non-collision damage like wildlife strikes and falling trees — common in the White Mountains. We help document your claim correctly to minimize adjuster delays.
Our digital triage collects key information about your damage — impact location, visible deformation, panel count, and whether airbags deployed — before you visit a shop. This ensures you're matched with a specialist equipped for your specific repair type, saving time and preventing mismatched shop assignments.
Minor cosmetic damage may take 2–4 days. Moderate repairs (panel replacements, paint) typically need 1–2 weeks. Major structural work can take 3–6 weeks depending on parts availability and damage extent.
